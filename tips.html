Tips and Advice

Local Customs and Etiquette

  • Australians are generally friendly and easygoing.
  • It's customary to greet with a simple "G'day" or "Hello."
  • Tipping is not mandatory but is appreciated for good service (around 10% of the bill in restaurants).

Food

  • Try Australian cuisine, which includes dishes like Vegemite (a spread for bread), meat pies, lamington, kangaroo, and damper (a type of bread).
  • Don't miss the opportunity to experience a traditional Australian barbecue (barbie).
  • Australia also offers diverse international cuisine due to its multicultural population, so you can find a wide variety of food options.

Wildlife and Nature

  • Australia is known for its unique wildlife. Be cautious around animals, as some can be dangerous. Follow safety guidelines, especially in national parks.
  • Do not feed or approach wild animals, as it can be harmful to both you and the animals.

Transportation

  • Brisbane has a well-connected public transport system, including buses, trains, and ferries. Consider purchasing a "Go Card" for convenient access.
  • If you plan to rent a car, remember that Australians drive on the left side of the road.

Emergency

  • Call 000 for police, fire, or medical assistance.

Indigenous Culture

  • Learn about and respect Australia's Indigenous cultures and traditions. Consider visiting cultural centers or attending Indigenous events to gain a deeper understanding.

By following these tips and being respectful of local customs and cultures, you can have a memorable and enjoyable visit to Australia during the 2032 Brisbane Olympics. Enjoy the games and the beautiful Australian landscape!
